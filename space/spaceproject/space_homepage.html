Artemis ll

Launch date:April 1, 2026, at 22:35:12 UTC from Kennedy Space Center, LC-39B.

End of mission:April 11, 2026, at 00:07:27 UTC.

Mission duration:9 days, 1 hour, 32 minutes, and 15 seconds.

Distance travelled:Approximately 694,481 miles (1,117,659 km)

Artemis II, also known as Exploration Mission-2 (EM-2), was a crewed lunar flyby mission operated by NASA . It marked the first human flight beyond low Earth orbit since Apollo 17 in 1972 and the second flight of the Space Launch System (SLS) alongside the first crewed flight of the Orion spacecraft, named Integrity by the crew. The mission aimed to test deep space systems, validate Orion for human spaceflight, and prepare for subsequent missions, including Artemis III, intended for lunar surface exploration NASA.

During the mission, the crew set several milestones: Victor Glover became the first person of color, Christina Koch the first woman, Jeremy Hansen the first non-U.S. citizen, and Reid Wiseman the oldest human to travel beyond low Earth orbit. They also set a human distance record from Earth of 252,756 miles (406,771 km), surpassing Apollo 13.
