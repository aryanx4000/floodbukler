Things To Do Before Floods.

→ Know Your Risk & Stay Informed

Check if your area is at risk using local flood maps on resources form authorities. Flooding can happen even away from rivers due to heavy rain or drainage issues.

Sign up for local emergency alerts, weather apps, radio or SMS services or your district warning system.

Learn evacuation routes, nearby higher ground, & shleters locations in advance. Pracice them with your family.




→ Make A Family Emergency plan

Discuss and write down what each family member will do: where to meet if separated, how to communicate(phones may fail), and who will handle pets or elderly members.

Include specail needs like medications, baby supplies, or mobility aids.

Plan for flash floods by identifying quick routes to high ground.




Build An Emergency Kit (Go-Bag)

Water:At least 5 litres per person per day(drinking, cooking, basic hygiene).

Food:Non-pershiable items(dry rations, biscuts, readt-to-eat foods) taht need little or no cooking.

Essentials:Battery-powered torch/flashlight, extra-batteries, hand-crank or battery radio, first-aid kit, essentials medicines(7-day supply), chlorine tablets for water purificaiton, candles/matches(in waterproof container), knife, thick ropes, waterproof footwear, rain gear, towels/blankets.

Documents:Copies of ID cards, health/vaccination records, insurance papers.

Others:Cash, Phone charger/power banks, pet supplies(if needed), sanitaion items(soap, snitizer, garbage-bags)




Prepare Your Home & Property

Elevate important items or move them to higher floors/attics if time allows.

Know how to shut off gas, electricity, & water mains to prevent fires, electrocution or leaks.

Clean gutters & drais=ns around your home to reduce water buildup.

Untie livestock or move pets to safety.




Get insurance & Document Everything

Standard home insurance often doesn't cover floods. Check or buy specific flood insurance well in advance.

Take photos/videos of your home interior's exterior, and valuable for insurance claims later.

Avoid building or storing valuables in basements/flood prone spots if possible

Things To Do During Floods.

Stay Informed & Follow Official Advice

Listen continuously to radio, TV, or reliable apps for updates.

Heed flood warnings immediately. If an evacuation order is issued, leave right away-do not wait for water to rise.

Avoid rumors: rely only on Official sources.




Move To Higher Ground immediately

If flooding starts or a flash flood is possible, go to the highest level in your building or to nearby high ground/ shelters. Don't wait for instrucitons.

If Trapped Indoors:Move to an upper floor or roof. Take your emergency kit, important documents, nedicines, water, and non-perishable food.




If You Must Evacuate

Secure your home quickly if time allows: Tuen off electricity at the main switch, close gas valves, an unplug appliances.

Take your Go-Bag with you.

Use sturdy footweaar and a stick/pole to test water depth if you must walk in still water




Health & Hygiene During The Food

USe disinfectants to clean surroudings if possible.

Protect against mosquitoes as stagnant water breeds them, increasing risks of diseases like dengue or malaria.

IF someone is sick or injured, seek medical help immediately- contact local helplines.

Check for gas leaks, strucural damage, and contamination brfore entering if safe. If you smell gas or see sparks, evaacuate immediately and call emergency services.




Things To Do After Flood

Wait For Official Clearcance & Ensure Safety First

Do not return home until authorities declare the area safe. Floodwaters can hide dangers like weakend strucures, sinkholes, or live wires.

Turn off electricity and gas at the main switch before entering if safe. Have a qualified electrician inspect wiring and appliances-never turn power on if standing in water or if lines are damaged.



Cleanup Safely

Remove mud and debris- Shovel out as much as possible, then hose down hard surfaces. Dispose of contamninted waste properly.

Throw away anything porous that touched floodwater, mattresses, pillows, upholstered furniture, carpets, drywalls bwlow water level andd food/perishables. These can harbor mold and bacteria



Health and Hygiene Precautions Drink only boiled, chlorinated or purified water. Do not use tap water until declared safe.

Discard any food taht contacted floodwater. Cook fresh meals; keep food covered.

Floods increase risks of waterborne(diarrohea, cholera, typhoid, hepatiits A), vector borne(dengue, malaria from stagant water), skin infections, lelptospirosis and resiratory issues.

Seek free medical aid at relief camps or govt. facilities.




Recovery & Rehabilitaion

Get water,electricity and sanitation services restored safely. Check sewage lines for backups.

Prioritize elderly, children, disabled and preganant individuals.

Report crop/livestock losses or business damage for compensation.
